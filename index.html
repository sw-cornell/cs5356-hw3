What I Learned About backdrop-filter

I used the backdrop-filter property to apply a blur effect to the background of the <header> element. Here's what I learned about its behavior:

  • Background-Only Filtering: The backdrop-filter property applies visual effects (like blur, brightness, or contrast) only to the background of an element, without affecting its content. This makes it perfect for creating modern UI effects like frosted glass.
  • Requires Transparency: For backdrop-filter to work, the element must have a transparent or semi-transparent background. In my code, I used background-color: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.5) to achieve this.
  • Blur Effect: I used backdrop-filter: blur(10px) to create a blur effect on the <header> background. The higher the blur value, the more pronounced the effect.

Overall, backdrop-filter is a powerful and easy-to-use property for enhancing the visual appeal of a website without altering the content.
